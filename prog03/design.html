Elements of Design

Introduction

"Good composition is like a suspension bridge - each line adds strength and takes none away. -Robert Henri"

Elements of design are the components that are used to design visual compositions. They form the building blocks of many projects that span across many creative fields like web design or painting. Understanding these elements and their interactions helps to produce more aesthetic and cohesive designs.

Being able to understand these elements help designers to become more efficient with communicating their ideas through visuals. This page will provide an overview of each element.

Line

A line is a continuous mark that connects 2 points. You can use lines to direct the viewer's eyes.

Types of Lines:

  • Straight
  • Curved
  • Zigzag
  • Dotted

Lines could also be literal such as a telephone line. They can also be implied and come from the borders of different colors, patterns, or even from motion.

Shape

Shape is a 2-dimensional area surrounded by an outline.

Types of Shapes:

  • Organic - Seen in nature
  • Geometric - Angular and mathematic
  • Abstract - Representational of things in nature

Color

Color is very important for creating the mood or tone of a piece. Color has different properties: hue, brightness/chroma, and value. Hue is the name of the color. Brightness is the intensity or saturation. Value is how light or dark it is.

Texture

Texture is how you would describe something if you could touch it. You can have physical texture in something like a sculpture that uses metal or another real material. You could also have optical texture which involves creating the illusion of texture through something like a painting or a photograph.

Value

Value is how light or dark something is. White would be the highest value while black would be the lowest. Different colors will also have different values. Value is important for creating contrast.

Form

Imagine form as the upgraded version of shape. Forms are 3-Dimensional objects like a sphere or a cube. Forms include volume, length, width, and height. Forms can be used to create the illusion that you are look at something with depth or dimension.

Space

Space is the area found between different elements. Positive space is filled with more elements while negative space is empty. Balanced use of space helps to make sure that designs don't feel cluttered or noisy. Space can be used to highlight important elements and create harmony.
